# Business Case

## The Decision

Meridian Financial Group has not been fined. No breach tied to non-human identity sprawl has occurred, publicly, yet. The board is being asked to fund identity governance architecture before an examination finding forces the conversation, or before one of 4 million ungoverned credentials becomes the entry point for an incident that does force it.

That is the right time to have this conversation. Not after the exam. Not after the incident. Now, while the choice is still Meridian's to make instead of a regulator's to dictate.

## The Cost of Doing Nothing

Meridian manages $340 billion in assets across retail banking, wealth management, and institutional trading. An examination finding citing inadequate identity and access controls does not simply result in a warning letter. It results in mandated remediation timelines, increased examination frequency, and in serious cases, restrictions on specific operational activities, restrictions that directly affect an institution operating at Meridian's scale and complexity.

Beyond the regulatory cost, every one of the 4 million ungoverned non-human identities in Meridian's environment is a live exposure. Industry data shows machine identities, not human accounts, are now the dominant factor in identity-related breaches, and firms that cannot demonstrate lifecycle governance and least-privilege enforcement for non-human identities are accumulating both security exposure and compliance exposure simultaneously. A single compromised, unrotated credential connected to a trading system or a compliance reporting pipeline does not need to be sophisticated to cause damage. It only needs to still be valid, and at Meridian's current state, the overwhelming majority of them are.

The regulatory clock compounds this. Notification windows as short as 36 to 72 hours assume an institution can produce a unified access picture on demand. Meridian currently cannot produce that picture in any timeframe, let alone one measured in hours, which means every day this gap persists is a day Meridian is one incident away from missing a mandatory notification deadline it has no ability to meet.

## The Investment

The non-human identity governance architecture is delivered in three phases, mirroring the discovery, lifecycle automation, and evidence layers described in the To-Be Architecture.

Phase 1, Discovery and Inventory, brings Meridian's 4 million fragmented, invisible identities into a single, continuously updated system of record. This is the foundation every subsequent phase depends on, and the phase with the fastest visible return, since Meridian goes from having no answer to having a complete, current answer to what exists.

Phase 2, Lifecycle and Ownership Automation, assigns accountable ownership and enforces credential expiration across the full identity population, eliminating the standing, indefinite credentials that represent Meridian's largest single source of unmanaged risk.

Phase 3, Policy Engine and Evidence Generation, deploys the deterministic enforcement and continuous evidence layer that turns Meridian's policy into something a regulator can actually verify, closing the specific gap examiners are citing across the industry.

The investment required is a fraction of the cost of a single adverse examination finding, when remediation mandates, increased examination frequency, and reputational cost to institutional clients are considered together.

## The Expected Benefits

**Regulatory benefit**
A demonstrable, continuously enforced identity governance architecture directly addresses the exact finding category regulators are citing industry-wide. Clean examination results reduce both the immediate remediation burden and the long-term cost of heightened regulatory scrutiny.

**Security benefit**
Eliminating standing, unrotated credentials and unowned identities closes the single largest and fastest-growing attack surface in Meridian's environment, one that current industry data shows is already the dominant vector in identity-related breaches across financial services.

**Operational benefit**
Automated discovery, lifecycle management, and evidence generation replace a manual review process that was never going to scale past a small fraction of Meridian's actual identity population. This is not a headcount increase to keep pace with growth. It is a structural fix that scales without proportional staffing cost.

**Institutional trust benefit**
Correspondent banks, institutional clients, and counterparties increasingly evaluate a financial institution's security posture as part of their own risk management. A demonstrable, audit-ready identity governance program is a competitive differentiator in institutional relationships where trust is the entire basis of the business.

## The Recommendation

Fund the three-phase non-human identity governance architecture.

The alternative is not the status quo. The alternative is walking into an examination with 4 million ungoverned identities and no way to answer the first question the examiner asks. Every quarter this is delayed is a quarter closer to that conversation happening on the regulator's terms instead of Meridian's own.

The architecture is built to scale with Meridian's continued adoption of AI-driven trading and compliance systems, not to be rebuilt the next time identity volume grows. What is approved now is the foundation Meridian's identity governance runs on for years, not a patch that buys another examination cycle.

This is not a cost of compliance. It is the difference between an institution that can answer a regulator's question in minutes and one that is still assembling spreadsheets when the deadline passes.
